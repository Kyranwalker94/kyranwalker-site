IRL 3D

3D Printer Customisation 🖨️

Upgrading two Creality Ender 3 V2 printers with silent cooling, custom printed parts, and aftermarket add-ons to improve print quality and reliability.

About the project

In 2021 I was running two Creality Ender 3 V2 printers. Both are capable machines out of the box, but out of the box means noisy fans, a few frustrating quirks, and plenty of headroom for improvement. This project was about making both machines quieter, more reliable, and better suited to longer unattended print runs.

The upgrade path combined off-the-shelf parts, better fans, upgraded extruder hardware, with custom 3D printed mounts, cable guides, and tool holders designed and printed on the machines themselves.

Fan upgrades

The stock fans on the Ender 3 V2 are functional but loud. Swapping them for quality silent alternatives makes a significant difference in noise floor, particularly during overnight prints.

  • Mainboard cooling fan replaced with a Noctua NF-A4x10 5V
  • Hotend cooling fan replaced with a quiet 4010 blower
  • Part cooling fan upgraded for improved airflow and reduced noise
  • All fan connectors checked and re-pinned where needed

Custom 3D printed additions

One of the pleasures of owning a 3D printer is printing improvements for the printer itself. Both machines received a set of printed upgrades designed to tidy up the workspace and improve usability.

  • Cable chain and drag chain routing along the X and Y axes
  • Tool and spare parts holder mounted to the frame
  • Screen angle bracket for better viewing angle
  • Filament guide and runout sensor mount
  • Raspberry Pi enclosure for OctoPrint remote control

Purchased upgrades

  • Dual Z-axis leadscrew kit, eliminated Z wobble on both machines
  • Metal extruder arm, replaced the brittle stock plastic arm
  • Capricorn PTFE bowden tube, reduced stringing and improved retraction
  • PEI spring steel print surface, replaced glass bed, massively improved first layer adhesion and part removal
  • BLTouch auto bed levelling sensor, removed the need for manual tramming

Results

After upgrades, both printers run noticeably quieter and produce more consistent prints. The dual Z-axis and BLTouch combination eliminated the majority of first-layer issues. OctoPrint added remote monitoring and the ability to cancel failed prints without being in the room.
