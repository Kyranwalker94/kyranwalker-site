3D Animation

Sovereign Harbour 🏗️

3D modelling and animation of the Sovereign Harbour area in Eastbourne, built in SketchUp, rendered in Blender and SimLab.

About the project

During 2020 I was learning as much about 3D modelling and animation as I could. Living in the Sovereign Harbour area of Eastbourne at the time, I decided to model the area and use it to test out different scene-building and animation techniques.

All scenes and the majority of models were built in SketchUp, then exported to Blender and SimLab for animation and rendering.

Animation 1: Blender

A short animation made in Blender, the concept was to eventually become an animated TV show opening credits sequence. This final draft doesn't yet feature characters, but shows what I learned about animating objects, moving cameras, and tracking subjects through a scene.

Blender animation, exterior scene with camera movement

Animation 2: SimLab (Interactive / VR)

This second animation shows the interior of my flat at the time, complete with furniture models. Imported into SimLab, I was able to assign click-triggered animations to objects like doors, making it possible to walk through the space as if it were a video game. The demo also ran in VR.

SimLab interactive walkthrough, VR compatible

What I learned

  • SketchUp for architectural modelling from scratch
  • Exporting and lighting scenes in Blender
  • Camera movement and object animation in Blender
  • Interactive 3D walkthroughs in SimLab
  • VR-ready scene setup
