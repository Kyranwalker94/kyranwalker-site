The Trip 2025 🚙❤️

A month-long road trip through Europe in the rebuilt Defender — twelve cities, six countries, Christmas markets, and the trip that ended with us getting married in New Zealand.

The idea

The Defender build had always had a goal behind it: not just to restore a truck, but to actually use it for something worthy. In November 2025 — with the restoration complete and the vehicle sorted — we loaded up and set off from the south of England on a month-long road trip through Europe.

The timing was deliberate. November in Europe means Christmas markets, mulled wine, fairy lights strung between medieval buildings, and a particular kind of magic that's hard to manufacture any other way. The plan was simple: drive, explore, and see what happened.

What happened was she said yes.

The route

We crossed the Channel and made our way through twelve cities across six countries — a roughly circular route taking in the best of northern and central Europe as the Christmas market season opened.

  • Amiens, France — Gothic cathedral, quiet canals, and the first night abroad
  • Tournai, Belgium — one of Belgium's oldest cities, stunning Romanesque tower
  • Brussels, Belgium — Grand Place lit up for Christmas, Belgian beer, waffles
  • Middelburg, Netherlands — beautiful Zeeland capital with a medieval abbey
  • Vlissingen, Netherlands — harbour town on the Zeeland coast, North Sea views
  • Colmar, France — the Alsatian jewel, possibly the most beautiful Christmas market in Europe
  • Konstanz, Germany — right on the Swiss border, Lake Constance in the fog
  • Munich, Germany — the Christkindlmarkt at Marienplatz, the Hofbräuhaus, snow
  • Bamberg, Germany — smoked beer, a UNESCO old town, and one of the best days of the trip
  • Venlo, Netherlands — crossing back north through the Dutch borderlands
  • Luxembourg City, Luxembourg — fairy-lit gorge, Christmas market in the old town
  • Reims, France — champagne caves, a gothic cathedral, and the last stop before home

The Defender

The trip was everything the restoration was built for. The Defender handled a month of driving through France, Belgium, Germany, the Netherlands, Luxembourg, and back without complaint — loaded with gear, sleeping equipment, and everything needed for a month on the road. It ran perfectly.

There's something satisfying about building a vehicle with your own hands and then taking it across a continent. Every mile felt earned.

What came next

We came home, regrouped, and in March 2026 we flew to New Zealand and got married. New Zealand had always been meaningful — I'd done a working holiday there in 2017 and it changed the direction of my life. Going back to get married felt right.

The trip was the beginning of the rest of it.
