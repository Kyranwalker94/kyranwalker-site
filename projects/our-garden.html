IRL In Progress

Our Garden 🌱

Renovating a neglected garden in 2026 — clearing, designing, and building it out with the goal of growing as much food as possible this year.

The project

After a few years of limited outdoor space, moving to a house with a proper garden in Hailsham in 2025 gave us the opportunity to actually use it. The garden came overgrown and underused — plenty of potential but in need of significant work before anything productive could happen.

The plan for 2026 is ambitious: clear what's there, design the space properly, build raised beds, and get as much growing as possible by the end of the season. This page will track progress as it happens.

The plan

  • Full clearance of overgrown areas — brambles, weeds, and unwanted growth
  • Layout planning — zoning the space for growing, seating, and paths
  • Raised bed construction — timber frames for vegetables and herbs
  • Soil preparation and composting setup
  • Grow list planning — prioritising crops that are expensive to buy or better fresh
  • Water butt installation for sustainable watering

Grow list 2026

The focus this year is on productive crops that justify the effort — things that are noticeably better home-grown, expensive at the supermarket, or just satisfying to produce.

  • Tomatoes — multiple varieties, both indoor and outdoor
  • Courgettes — prolific producers, easy to manage
  • Lettuce and salad leaves — fast growing, cut-and-come-again
  • Runner beans and French beans
  • Herbs — basil, parsley, chives, mint
  • Strawberries — long term planting for future seasons
  • Potatoes — if space allows

Progress log

Phase 1 — Clearance In Progress

Initial clearance of the main beds. Removing brambles, levelling the ground, and assessing what's worth keeping.

Phase 2 — Raised beds Planned

Building timber raised beds and filling with a topsoil and compost mix ready for planting.

Phase 3 — First plantings Planned

Getting seeds and seedlings in the ground — starting indoors where needed and transplanting out after last frost.

This project is actively in progress — more detail, photos, and progress updates will be added throughout 2026 as the garden develops.
