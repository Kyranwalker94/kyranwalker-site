IRL Travel

Round the World 2017 🌍

An 18-month journey that covered four continents, three months volunteering with big cats in South Africa, two weeks trekking the Andes in Peru, a road trip from Wyoming to Key West, and nine months living in New Zealand. The trip that changed everything.

South Africa

The trip started in South Africa with three months volunteering at a reserve for abandoned and rescued big cats. Lions, cheetahs, and other large predators that had been taken from the wild or from captivity, too habituated to humans to be released, living out their lives on the reserve.

The work was hands-on, feeding, enclosure maintenance, and general upkeep of the reserve. You were never far from animals that could hurt you if you weren't paying attention. That combination of responsibility and proximity to something genuinely wild was unlike anything I'd experienced before.

Three months of that recalibrates what you consider normal. Getting on a plane to South America afterwards felt comparatively ordinary.

South America

Two weeks in Ecuador and Peru, which isn't long enough, but long enough to leave a mark.

The main event was the Salkantay trail in Peru, a four-day high-altitude trek through the Andes with a guide, one of the alternative routes to Machu Picchu that avoids the crowds and goes through proper mountain terrain. Condors overhead. Glaciated peaks. The kind of scenery that doesn't look real from the valley floor.

  • Salkantay trail completed in 4 days with a local guide
  • Condors spotted in flight above the high passes
  • Bright colourful markets full of local produce and handmade goods
  • Tried cuy, traditional roasted guinea pig, a Peruvian staple

South America felt completely alive. Loud markets, vivid colour everywhere, food being cooked and sold on every corner. Two weeks was barely enough to scratch the surface.

USA

Three months in the States, and easily the most varied leg of the trip.

It started in Las Vegas with three days with Cole, a friend I'd known for years who had family and connections across the country. Vegas was exactly what it needed to be for three days. Then we got a Greyhound to Denver, which is a long way to sit on a bus but there's something about watching the American desert go by through a coach window that earns its place as a memory.

From Denver we flew into Wyoming on a tiny propeller plane that made the landing feel like an achievement. We drove out to Crowheart, a remote area of Wyoming, where Cole's dad and friends were based.

Wyoming and the wilderness

A week in the Wyoming wilderness learning survival skills from Cole's dad and his group. How to navigate, how to read terrain, how to find water and food, how to build shelter. Real practical knowledge, passed on by people who actually use it, in country that would make the need for it obvious.

The landscape out there is something else entirely: open, massive, and indifferent to you in a way that cities never are. Four days of camping and hiking in North Colorado followed, Cole and I on our own in the mountains.

The road trip

Cole's dad drove us from Crowheart, Wyoming to Key West, Florida. Non-stop. I'm not sure of the exact hours but it was the kind of drive where you lose track of days. The country goes from sage and scrub to green and humid and then tropical before you run out of road at the southern tip of Florida.

We stopped at Disney World en route for Cole's sister, which was a surreal detour from everything else the trip had been. Then Key West itself: a week on a US military base, friends of Cole's dad stationed there. The whole week felt like a different world, the southernmost point of the continental USA, boats and heat and the Atlantic and the Gulf meeting at the same strip of road.

Cole's sister bought a cat in Florida for the drive back. It was not happy about the journey and made sure we all knew it. The whole return route smelled accordingly.

Los Angeles

The American leg ended in Los Angeles. A day walking Venice Beach and Santa Monica Pier before catching the flight west. The Pacific looking exactly like it should, the pier exactly as iconic as it appears in every film you've seen it in. Then the long flight to Auckland.

New Zealand

Nine months. The longest and most significant leg of the trip, and the one I didn't fully understand the importance of until much later.

I lived in Auckland with Cole and Amira, who would eventually become my wife. We worked, we cooked, we had a lot of fun with a group of friends that formed around us. Auckland is a good city for that: small enough that you keep running into the same people, big enough that there's always somewhere new to go.

  • Road trip around the Coromandel Peninsula, hot water beaches, native forest, coastal bays
  • Hobbiton movie set in Matamata, remarkably unchanged from the films
  • Waitomo Glowworm Caves, ancient limestone caverns lit from the inside by thousands of luminescent larvae
  • Never made it to the South Island, which remains unfinished business

At the time it felt like an extended adventure. Looking back, it was the period that set the direction for everything that followed. The people I met there, Amira most of all, the way of thinking about what life could look like, the sense that there was no obligation to do things in the order everyone else expected.

I came back to England in mid-2018, started working at Plummer Parsons in July, and began building something more structured. But the trip is the context behind all of it. We went back to New Zealand in March 2026 to get married.
